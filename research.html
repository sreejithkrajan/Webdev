Integrated passives and packaging design, fabrication and testing for mm sized neural implants

  • PIDr. Bakir
  • TimespanMar. 2017 Onwards

Developing reliable brain machine interfaces (BMIs) is of prime importance in understanding many neurological disorders like Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s disease. It can also help restore sensory and motor abilities in patients suffering from such deficits, caused by traumatic injuries, neurological diseases, or loss of limbs. Most of the clinically viable versions suffer from the inability to perform long-term, high spatio-temporal resolution recording over large neuronal populations. We seek to address this issue by creating mm-sized neural probes, which can be implanted in large numbers across the brain’s area of interest.


The main challenges being addressed in this project to take the distributed free-floating neural recording system from concept to a clinically viable system are:


  • Developing a wireless power/data transfer mechanism for the implants
  • Creating reliable and minimally invasive hermetic packages to ensure the longevity of the probes
  • ASIC design with low power/size constrains
  • Neurosurgical procedures

We achieve this by separating the ASIC and package design by using 3D integration techniques. The current work focuses on design and development of high Q MEMS inductors and high density integrated capacitors for the power/ data transfer. The work is done in collaboration with Dr. Maysam Ghovanloo’s group, GT Bionics. Kindly refer to my published work for more details and updates.
